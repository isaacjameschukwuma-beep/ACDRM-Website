The GIE Nexus Model: A New Blueprint for African Emergency Governance

ACDRM Editorial 2026 6 min read

Across Africa, disaster risk is too often treated as a series of emergencies to be managed rather than a governance challenge to be solved. ACDRM's Governance-Interoperability-Ethics (GIE) Nexus Model reframes the problem, and offers a practical roadmap for institutional reform.

Why Governance Comes First

Strong institutional frameworks, policy coherence, and coordinated leadership are the structure on which all resilience depends. Without governance, even well-resourced systems fragment under pressure. The model treats governance as the foundation, not an afterthought.

Africa does not lack resilience. It lacks the institutional systems to organise and sustain it.

Interoperability in Practice

Disaster risk management demands seamless collaboration across government, humanitarian, and development ecosystems. Interoperability means systems, agencies, and actors that communicate, coordinate, and deliver together, not in silos.

Ethics as the Foundation of Trust

Accountability, transparency, and the protection of vulnerable populations sit at the centre of every decision. Ethical governance is not optional; it is what earns and sustains institutional trust.

  • Governance, institutional frameworks and coordinated leadership.
  • Interoperability, systems and actors that work together.
  • Ethics, accountability and protection of the vulnerable.

Together, these three pillars define how ACDRM designs every engagement, structures every programme, and measures every outcome, ensuring the work builds institutions and systems that last.
